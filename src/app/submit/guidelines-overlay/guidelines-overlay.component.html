Welcome to Poems India!

We're excited to read your work! Here's everything you need to know about submitting.

General Guidelines

• All submissions must be original work by the author

• Previously published work will not be accepted

• Please ensure your work is properly proofread before submission

• We accept simultaneous submissions but please notify us if your work is accepted elsewhere

• Response times:

- Poetry & Prose: 3 weeks

- Articles & Cinema Essays: 1 week

- Opinion pieces: 24-48 hours

Content-Specific Guidelines

Poetry

  • • Submit up to 3 poems per submission
  • • Any style or form welcome
  • • No maximum line limit per poem

Articles

  • • Minimum 1,000 words (no maximum limit)
  • • Well-researched and fact-checked
  • • Include citations for sources

Cinema Essays

  • • Minimum 1,000 words (no maximum limit)
  • • Focus on film analysis and critique
  • • Academic or journalistic style

Prose

  • • No word limit restrictions
  • • Fiction or creative non-fiction
  • • Strong narrative voice
  • • Complete stories preferred

Opinion

  • • Minimum 500 words
  • • Personal perspectives and viewpoints
  • • Contemporary issues and topics
  • • Fast track: 24-48 hours response

Submission Process

  1. 1 Complete the submission form and submit your work
  2. 2 Track your submission status through "My Submissions" in your profile
  3. 3 Our editorial team will carefully evaluate your submission
  4. 4 Upon acceptance, you'll receive an email notification
  5. 5 If revisions are requested, please respond within 72 hours

Rights & Legal

• By submitting, you confirm that you own the rights to your work

• You retain copyright to your work

• If accepted, we request first publication rights

• You may withdraw your submission at any time before publication

• We reserve the right to make minor editorial changes

Editorial Guidelines

Authentic Voice

Poems that sound like an actual person, not "A Poet" - with natural speech patterns and genuine personality.

Lived Specificity

Concrete details from real experience - your grandmother's exact kitchen, not generic "elderly wisdom."

Productive Messiness

Beautiful imperfection where language struggles in interesting ways, creating unexpected meaning.

Risk-Taking

Willingness to fail interestingly - whether through form, subject, or approach.

Emotional Complexity

Feelings that resist easy labels - love mixed with resentment, joy tinged with fear.

Fresh Imagery

Original metaphors that surprise rather than decorate; connections we haven't seen before.

Cultural Specificity

Details that reveal your actual world, background, and lived experience.

Natural Line Breaks

Breaks that serve meaning and breath, not just making text look "poetic."

Earned Difficulty

Complexity that serves the poem's needs, not showing off.

Surprising Turns

Moments that surprise even you as the writer; unexpected directions that feel inevitable in hindsight.

AI-Generated Perfection

Technically flawless but emotionally hollow - smooth, safe, and soulless.

Manufactured Metaphors

Forced comparisons chosen for cleverness rather than truth or emotional resonance.

Metaphor Overload

Too many competing images that muddle rather than clarify meaning.

Direct Plagiarism

Copying others' work - we check, and we recognize lifted lines and concepts.

Concept Theft

Stealing unique structural ideas or approaches while changing surface words.

Clichéd Language

Roses are red territory; hearts like glass; dancing in rain; time healing wounds.

Purple Prose

Overly ornate language prioritizing "sounding poetic" over actual communication.

Vague Abstraction

Heavy use of "soul," "beauty," "eternity" without concrete grounding.

Forced Rhyme

Distorting natural syntax or choosing weaker words just to maintain rhyme scheme.

Emotional Telling

Declaring feelings ("I was sad") instead of creating experiences that evoke them.

Heavy-Handed Symbolism

Over-explaining obvious symbols; not trusting reader intelligence.

Inconsistent Voice

Switching between registers without purpose; losing yourself trying to sound literary.

Template Poetry

Filling in Mad Libs-style structures; formulaic approaches to "deep" subjects.

Performative Depth

Generic profundity that sounds meaningful but says nothing specific.

Questions?

We're here to help! If you have any questions about submitting your work, please feel free to reach out:

Email: poemsindia@gmail.com
Response time: We typically respond to inquiries within 2-3 business days
